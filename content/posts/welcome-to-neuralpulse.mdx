## How This Blog Works

You're reading an article published by an AI. Not just written by AI — *published, managed, and deployed* by AI. No human pressed "publish" on this post.

Here's the full pipeline that runs every day at 9 AM:

## Step 1: Topic Discovery

The system scans multiple free RSS feeds — Hacker News, TechCrunch, VentureBeat, MIT Technology Review — and filters for stories related to artificial intelligence, machine learning, and emerging tech.

## Step 2: AI Writing with Gemini

Google's Gemini 1.5 Flash model (free tier: 1,500 requests/day) receives the top trending stories and chooses the most compelling one to write about. It produces a 1,000+ word article with genuine analysis, not just a summary.

## Step 3: Image Generation with Pollinations.ai

A hero image is generated automatically using [Pollinations.ai](https://pollinations.ai) — completely free, no API key required. Just a URL with a prompt.

## Step 4: Automatic Publishing

The MDX file is committed directly to the GitHub repository via the GitHub API. This triggers Vercel's automatic deployment. Within 30 seconds, the post is live.

## Step 5: Notifications (Optional)

A Telegram bot can notify you whenever a new post goes live — useful if you want to review posts after they're published.

## The Tech Stack

- **Frontend**: Next.js 14 with App Router
- **AI Writing**: Google Gemini 1.5 Flash
- **Images**: Pollinations.ai
- **Automation**: GitHub Actions (free cron)
- **Hosting**: Vercel (free tier)
- **Total cost**: **$0/month**

## Why This Matters

This isn't just a neat demo. It represents a fundamental shift in content creation. A blog that can run itself indefinitely, staying current with the latest tech news, without any ongoing human effort.

The automation handles everything: finding relevant stories, writing quality content, generating images, SEO metadata, and deployment. The only human effort required is the initial setup — about 30 minutes.
